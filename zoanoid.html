27 Spectacular Western-Style Origami Dragons



Dragons are some of my favourite things that people make out of paper and
we recently did a post dedicated to Eastern-style origami dragons so
it’s time to do a round-up of some incredible looking Western-style dragons.

When we talk about Western-style dragons we’re talking about the sorts
of dragons that you see out of European myths and stories, the types of
dragons that you usually think of when you think of medieval fantasy settings.

First up is this great traditional looking dragon designed by Tetsuya Gotani.



Here is another great traditional sort of fantasy dragon design by Sergey Yartsev.

I really like the design of this next dragon, especially how it’s posed.

This is an excellent photo of Satoshi Kamiya’s Ancient Dragon flying away with a horse.

This next model is a wyvern which is a two legged dragon with a barbed tail.

Here’s how Satoshi Kamiya’s Ancient Dragon normally looks.

This Darkness Dragon has a similar shape to Satoshi Kamiya’s Ancient Dragon.

Here’s a great specifically Western dragon design by Shuki Kato.

The head of the dragon in this fantastic photo belongs to Satoshi Kamiya’s Ancient Dragon.

This is a unique hybrid-dragon designed by Rodolfo Rizzo.

Shuki Kato’s Zoanoid Dragon is one of my favourite papercraft dragons.

Here’s another great Ancient Dragon folded by Han-Sheng Yang.

Here’s a quite tiny and very impressive version of Nham Van Son’s paper wyvern.

Here’s Shuki Kato’s awesome Western Dragon folded by Shuki Kato himself.

This is a different and slightly more evil looking wyvern design by Philipp-Marius Kost.

This is another great and more “traditional” looking dragon design.
